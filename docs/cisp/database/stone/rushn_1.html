Names

  • Dovaidona (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Ziegler/1994, 169--170, states that this name is an n-stem in the genitive singular. She sees the element DOV(A) giving Old Irish dub and -AIDONA < *aido(n)-, with the whole name DOVAIDONA giving Old Irish Dubaéd. Cf. McManus/1991, 103, 105: 'black' and 'fire'.
  • Droata (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Stokes/1890 and Rhys/1890a give a full discussion of the name Droata with Stokes preferring this to be an error for Druada and Rhys seeing no problem with the form carved on the stone.

    Macalister/1902, 136: `Droata is probably more safely to be regarded as a proper name than as any form of the word for "druid".'

    Ziegler/1994, 172, suggests that DROATA is related to *drosat.

    Kermode/1907, 97 took DROATA to mean `Druid'.

  • Druta (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Ziegler/1994, 172, suggests this reading, rather than DROATA. She states that it is a genitive singular i-stem, related to DRUTIQULI [BAKNK/6]. She also sees Druta as a hypercorisitic form which would give Old Irish Drúith.
