Names

  • Bivaidonas (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Byrne/1973, 110, suggests that the person named was related to the Conailli Muirtheimne of north Louth and south Down.

    This identification is followed by Cubbon/1982, 259, and OCróinín/1995, 50.

    Ziegler/1994, 137--138, states that this name is an n-stem in the genitive singular. She also states that BIV(A)- < *biuo- gives Old Irish béo and -AID- gives aéd, with BIVAIDONAS giving Old Irish Béoaéd.

  • Cunava[li] (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Ziegler/1994, 161, states that CUNAVA[LI] gives Old Irish Conual, Conall, with the ogham form an o-stem in the genitive singular; cf. the tribal name Conaille, McManus/1991, 111. McManus points out that the structural congruence of British and Primitve Irish makes it difficult to establish whether the name is British or Irish in origin, or belongs independently to both; cf. British Cynwal, McManus/1991, 113.
