Language:Goidelic (ogham)
Ling. Notes:See Sims-Williams/1992, 40--42, for a full discussion of this inscription, and especially the use of the `St Andrew's cross' forfid for /p/.
Palaeography:Macalister/1945, 224: `the P being represented by the I-forfid.'

McManus/1991, 79, also notes the use of this symbol for the writing of p.

Sims-Williams/1992, 41--42, discusses the form of this p symbol, and argues that its `St Andrews cross' shape `can be interpreted as a BB monogram', illustrative of the closeness of B and P in Irish, Latin and some Continental Celtic languages.

Legibility:some

Names

  • Logitti (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    See McManus/1991, 108. For a similar example, LUGUTTI, see CRAG/1.
  • Erpenn (Language: Incomplete Information; Gender: male)
    Sims-Williams/1992, 40--41, having ruled out the possibility of equating this name with names in ERC-, goes on to state that `Macalister's attempt to see a Pict down here in Co. Kerry, citing the Pictish name Erp, seems a desperate but not impossible solution. ... it is not impossible that ERPENN should be a hybrid of Pictish Erp and Irish diminuitive -én. But this is quite uncertain'.
