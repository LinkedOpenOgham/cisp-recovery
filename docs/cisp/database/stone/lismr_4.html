Palaeography:Okasha/Forsyth/2001, 342: `Two modern letters, D and C, are incised in the top two quadrants around the cross. ... The [main] text ... uses half-uncial script of maximum letter H. 8.5 cm. As noted above, it appears that the letters have been spaced around the cross, presumably so that the cross-arm falls at a word division'. Macalister/1949, 108: 'The stone must have been adapted as a tomb stone at some recent date, when the initials D - C were cut in the two upper cantons'.

CISP: The lettering is Insular half-uncial. The first two Ds have ascenders which bend to the left over an open bow. The final D the the A before it are similar in form. Both have lozenge-shaped bows. In the case of the D the ascender continues above the bow while in the case of the A is continues below the bow with a curved extension to the right. The R is majuscule. Abbreviation mark above R.
