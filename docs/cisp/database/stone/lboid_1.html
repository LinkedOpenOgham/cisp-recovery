Names

  • Echadi (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Steer/1969, 129, referring to the name ECHODI, from Iona, and citing a personal comment from K.H. Jackson, states `linguistically the name would not be older than the later sixth century nor later than the quite early eighth. Echodi has the Latin genitive termination in -i. There are several characters Latinised as Echodius in Adamnan, and the genitive occurs once as Echudi. The genuine Irish (sixth/seventh-century) form of the nominative occurs in the Appendix to Adamnan, Echoid...the genitive in O.Ir was Echdach (Latin Echodi and Echudi are based on the nominative), the name must have been something like Equodix, genitive Equodicos, in primitive Celtic'.

    The name is Goidelic.
