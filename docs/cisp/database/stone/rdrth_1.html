Names

  • MAVORI (Language: Brittonic; Gender: male)
    Thomas/1985, 174: `The name Mavorius is originally a British one … and we can suppose a British *Mauuri-os, -us (the first -u- being the semi-consonantal -w- sound'.

    Thomas/1994, 284, suggests that the name is close to the Roman name Mavortius, or even Mavorius which he sees as Gaulish.

  • Mavouih (Language: Indeterminate; Gender: male)
    Reading by Okasha/1993, 219, who goes on to state that: `A Celtic name, probably reading MAVOHE[NI] occurs on a stone from Llanboidy, Wales'.
  • Vito[--] (Language: Latin; Gender: male)
    Okasha/1993, 219: `personal names beginning VIT- are well recorded, for example St Clement VITALI'.

    Thomas/1994, 284: `VITO... (for Vitori?)'.

    Thomas/1985, 174: `a late, rustic and erroneous inclusion of the Latin name VICTO(RI)'.
