Site Notes

Macalister/1945, 142: `Found in a peat-bog, at a depth of 6 or 7ft. Below the surface. According to Hitchcock, who had opportunities of questioning the finder, a man named P. McDonnel, the actual place of discovery was on the adjacent townland of Reask. In the same place were found a quern, an iron pot, something described as 'part of a child's cradle', and some charred wood'.
