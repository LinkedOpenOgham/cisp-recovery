Site Notes

Macalister/1945, 129: `Three inscribed stones stand around a carn which is a centre for pilgrimage and for paying of rounds. Lying on the top of the mound is a stone with a basin-shaped hollow, around which are the supporting sticks of invalids, said to have been relieved here of their infirmities'.
