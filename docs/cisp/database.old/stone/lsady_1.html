Names

  • Totavali (Language: celtic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 307, n 2, argues that the name may very likely be Irish. Jackson/1953, 645, also argues that the spelling TOTAVALI has the first -a- for -o-, as a mis-spelling of the composition vowel reflecting a slurring of the sound. Jackson/1953, 648, suggests some Brittonic comparanda for the name, including Welsh Tudwal. Thomas/1994, 94, 158 sees the name as British.
  • Dothoranti (Language: celtic; Gender: male)
    Thomas/1994, 94, 158, sees the name as Irish. Jackson/1953, 566, states that the name DOTHORANTI is unattested elsewhere. Harvey/1989, 56--66, has argued that the digraph -th- was an Irish invention to represent spirantization. This has been questioned by Russell/1996, who draws attention to names such as Theodoric from the other areas of Europe.
