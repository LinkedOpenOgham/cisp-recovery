Names

  • Diarmait (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Petrie/1878, 44: `There was a bishop of Iniscealtra in the early part of the tenth century, whose name was Dermot; but then his father's name was Caicher...There was a bishop of Inis Cathaigh (Scattery Island), near the mouth of the Shannon, who bore the name Diarmait, who lived in the eleventh century'.
  • Delbaid (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Petrie/1878, 44: `Delbaid (Dealbhait) is a name which occurs in the Annals as that of one of the Tuatha dé Denann, who overcame the Fir Bolg, Dealbhaeth, son of Ogma, A.M. 2544 (Chron. Scot. ); and a king of Ireland, A.M. 3451, 3460 (Four Masters)'.
