Names

  • Covagni (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    McManus/1991, 107, argues that the suffix -AGNI > AN(N) is diminuitive and also very common on Ogham inscriptions.

    McManus/1991, 111--112, further argues that this person, as a result of their gentilic, may have been a member of the Luigni of Meath.

    Ziegler/1994, 156, argues for this being the genitive singular of an O-stem with the diminutive suffix *-gno-, and that in Old Irish this name gave Cuan.

  • Luguni (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    McManus/1991, 108, sees this name as possibly having a nasal suffix and that Luguni gave Luigne.
