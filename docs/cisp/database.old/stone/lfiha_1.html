Language:Latin (rcaps)
Ling. Notes:The name of the commemorand is in the nominative, preceded by the formula HIC IACIT.
Paleo. Notes:Nash-Williams/1950, 115, 'Roman capitals...with horizontal final -I. FIVS is perhaps a contracted (blundered?) form of FILIVS'.

Macalister/1945, 353, 'Roman capitals...the FI is, as usual, ligatured. The omission of the following LI is presumably due to the lapidary's error'.

The two Gs are sickle-shaped, or half-uncial. The MA of SENOMAGLI are conjoined, but not ligatured, and the letters vary in height with the S being consistently the tallest and the OMA of SENOMAGLI the smallest.

Names

  • Ulcagnus (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 518, n. 1, sees Ulcagnus as Goidelic and related to the Irish name Olcan.
  • Senomaglos (Language: celtic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 518 note 1, argues that this name is 'probably' Goidelic on the basis that the name Ulcagnus on the same stone is Goidelic. However, on the previous page he cites SENEMAGLI as being British.
