Site:IONAS
Discovery:first mentioned, 1875 Dryden, H.
History:Formerly incorporated in the paving in front of St Columba's shrine.

Allen/Anderson/1903, 400, describes it as in the enclosure at the W end of the Cathedral (i.e. Abbey).

It was lying on the floor of the N transept of the `cathedral' (i.e. Abbey) when viewed by Macalister/1912.

Now in the Abbey Museum.

Names

  • Flann (Language: Goidelic; Gender: incomplete)
    OCorráin/Macguire/1991, 105: `bright red, blood red'...a very popular early Irish name', for both men and women.

    Lionard/1961, 126, 162, identifies this figure with Flann mac Maíl-dúin, abbot of Iona who died in 891, but the name is common, and the form of the script suggests 8th-century dating according to RCAHMS/1982, 187.
