Discovery:recognised, 1931 O'Sullivan, T.F.
History:OSullivan/1931, 398, is the first published record of this stone.

Cuppage/etal/1986, 253: `dislodged by a flood which affected the bed of a stream...the find place was near Kilfountan Church...preserved for a while in a farmhouse near Kildrum graveyard and then removed to the grounds of St. Brendan's Seminary in Killarney where it remains today.'

Macalister/1945, 162, states that the stone had `remained in the possession of the local farmer, one Murphy, and he presented it to the priest who attended him on his deathbed. The latter presented it to his bishop, and it is now erected at Killarney, in the grounds of St. Brendan's Seminary'.
