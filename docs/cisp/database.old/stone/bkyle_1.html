Ling. Notes:OhEailidhe/1967, 121, notes the unusual word-order of the inscription with the name preceeding the 'OROIT DO' formula. He also notes the unusual spelling of ORAI, only found elsewhere at KBRCN/7, or Macalister/1949, no. 538.
Paleo. Notes:OhEailidhe/1967, 121: `The use of the half-uncial S and the Roman minuscular d could be an indication of early date...Macalister in his study of the palaeography of the Clonmacnoise slabs, has noted that the alphabet which includes the half-uncial S and minuscular d, is characteristic...to the last half of the eighth century.' Also mentioned are the unusual ligature of U/S in the middle of a word, and the 'point-topped' A.
