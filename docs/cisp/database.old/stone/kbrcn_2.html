As the stone has been lost all assessments of the decoration are based upon the illustrations of Petrie/1878, Plate XIII.

Higgins1987, 368: `a rather elaborate cross of Latin form. The cross itself is ringed and has a three-line shaft and two single-line arms each of which terminates in a ringed cross. The upper arms have an encircled Greek cross. The form of the terminal at the top of the cross is unknown as the slab is damaged in this area. The shaft rises from a trapezoidal, two-line case, the bottom of which is missing due to damage. The junction of the arms and shaft has a central roundel intersected by the transom of the cross, surrounded by a concentric annular ring deriving from the shaft of the cross'.
