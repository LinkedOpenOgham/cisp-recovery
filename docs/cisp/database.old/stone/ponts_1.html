Discovery:first mentioned, 1694 Lhuyd, E.
History:Lhwyd/1910, 90: `On Ystrad mountain in ye parish of Landhetty'.

Nash-Williams/1950, 79: 'Sketched by or for Edward Llwyd. Now lost'. Webley/1957, 119: `The author rediscovered the stone while carrying out an archaeological survey in South Brecknockshire. It is sited not on the Ystrad Mountain but immediately below in the Cwm Criban valley'.

Dimensions:1.65 x 0.35 x 0.3 (converted from Webley/1957)
Setting:in ground
Location:unknown
Webley/1957, 119: `It is sited … in the Cwm Criban valley'. Webley/1957, 119, also gives the grid reference of O.S. 6-in. Sheet 46NW, 1-in. Sheet 141/073131.
Form:plain
Webley/1957, 119--120, refers to the stone as a 'pillar' with a 'pronounced shoulder' 22 inches from the top. Nash-Williams/1950, 79: `Rough pillar-stone, 'pitch'd on end a yard a.h. high, a foot thick, h. a yd. broad''.
