Names

  • Ercagni (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    White/1971--1972, 46-51, cites personal communications from K. Jackson and M. Richards, arguing that Ercagni is from Latin *Ercangus or British *Ercagnos giving Erchan in Welsh. White, 51, quotes Jackson as stating `the name of the dead man, Ercagnos, is certainly an Irish one, though this naturally does not mean that this particular man was Irish'. He also cites Richards as arguing that since *Erc and -agn- appear in both British and Irish, a choice of origin is not possible.

    Edwards/1986, 23 argues for the name's Irish nature.
