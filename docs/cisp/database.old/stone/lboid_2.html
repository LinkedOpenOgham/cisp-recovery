The M is of the `comb' variety, the A has an angle-bar, the H is half-uncial. The FI of FILI is conjoined, and the final I is horizontal. The N of LUNAR[..] is H-shaped, and the R has an open bow, and perhaps a short oblique stroke. In the third line we have another half-uncial H and two horizontal I's.

Names

  • Mavoheni (Language: Brittonic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 440: `The existence of Pr. W. *maw seems supported by...MAVOHE(NI), the later from presumably British *Magusenos'.

    Jackson/1953, 521, states that -HENI, was from *-senos, showing change from -s to -h. Jackson also translates the name as `Old Lad'.

  • Lunari Cocci (Language: Brittonic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 566--67, 570, argues that COCCI is a late example of the spelling cc in British, but that it 'very likely has CC spelt for ch. Jackson/1953, 570, one the basis of reading the name LUNARI, and not Nash-Williams/1950, 113, LUNAR[C?]HI, argues that this name cannot indicate the time by which rc had become rch, which is what Rhys/1879, 61, argues for.
