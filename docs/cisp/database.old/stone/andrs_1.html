Names

  • Ammecati (Language: Brittonic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 173, 187, 509, 621, 623, 645, argues that Ammecati is the British equivalent of Irish *Imbicatos, with the -mb- of *Ambecatos being assimilated to -mm- to give our form here. He argues that this is evidence for spoken British on Man.

    The element *catos means 'battle'.

  • Rocati (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 173, `*Rocatus [is a]...Goidelic name...[giving] Rochadh.'

    Jackson/1953, 187, `The name *Rocatus is either both British and Irish, in which case the C and T stand for g, d, and ch, th respectively; or is it Irish only, in which case the Latin C and T are spellings for ch and th'.

    The element *catos means 'battle'.

Names

  • [Am]b[i]catos (Language: Brittonic; Gender: male)
    The name Ambecatos is British, the Irish form being *Imbicatos. The Ogham form of it shows the influence of both British and Irish prnounciation - British -AM-, Irish -MB-.

    The element *catos means 'battle'.

  • Roc[a]t[o]s (Language: Goidelic; Gender: male)
    Jackson/1953, 187, argues that the name is the Primitive Irish form of Rochadh.

    It shows no trace of lenition, syncope or apocope. The element *catos means 'battle'.
